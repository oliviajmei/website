igvc

autonomous ground vehicle competition

mechanical design of the chassis, drivetrain, steering, and sensor packaging for an autonomous ground vehicle competition platform.

design timeline

a breakdown of the major subsystems developed for the igvc platform.

chassis design

built from 80/20 extrusion for modularity, rapid iteration, and straightforward subsystem integration. the chassis layout separates payload, battery, and electronics while leaving room for an acrylic enclosure and future design changes.

drivetrain

direct drive wheel architecture using custom machined motor mounts. this layout keeps the drivetrain compact, serviceable, and easier to manufacture while supporting a clean packaging strategy inside the frame.

steering system

ackermann-style steering with a custom rack housing, bushings, tie rods, motor mount, and steering knuckles. the assembly was designed to better reflect real vehicle steering behavior while remaining practical for student manufacturing.

sensor mounts

3d printed mounts designed to package the zed2 camera and lidar securely on the vehicle. the mounts prioritize alignment, fast installation, and easy replacement during testing.
