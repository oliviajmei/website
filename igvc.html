design timeline

Breakdown of the major mechanical systems developed for the IGVC platform, with alternating descriptions and visual callouts for each subsystem.

node 01

chassis design

Built from 80/20 extrusion for modularity, quick iteration, and durable subsystem mounting. The layered base structure separates payload, battery, and electrical systems while leaving room for an acrylic outer enclosure and future hardware changes.

node 02

drivetrain

Direct-drive wheel layout using custom motor mounts designed around manufacturability and clean packaging. This subsystem supports a compact drive architecture while keeping assembly straightforward and serviceable.

node 03

steering system

Ackermann-inspired steering geometry with a custom housing, bushings, tie rods, and steering knuckles. The layout was developed to better emulate real vehicle steering behavior while staying manufacturable for a student-built autonomous platform.

node 04

sensor mounts

Dedicated 3D-printed mounts designed to package the ZED2 camera and Helios LiDAR cleanly onto the chassis. The mounts prioritize stable alignment, simple fastening, and fast replacement during testing and iteration.
